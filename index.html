We introduce the GuideData Dataset, a collection of qualitative data, focusing on the interactions between guide dog trainers, visually impaired (BLV) individuals and their guide dogs. The dataset captures a variety of real-world scenarios, including navigating sidewalks, climbing stairs, crossing streets, and avoiding obstacles. By providing this comprehensive dataset, the project aims to advance research in areas such as assistive technologies, robotics, and human-robot interaction, ultimately improving the mobility and safety of visually impaired people.

Abstract

Research on mobile assistive systems for blind and low-vision (BLV) individuals spans five decades, beginning in 1976. However, mobile navigation systems that mimic how guide dogs assist BLV people remain rare. One critical missing component is the lack of understanding of how guide dogs and BLV handlers navigate real-world environments. In this paper, we address this gap by defining a guide-dogâ€“inspired navigation setup based on a comprehensive human study involving interviews with 26 guide dog handlers, 2 white cane users, 5 guide dog trainers, 1 O\&M trainer, 10 hours of observing guide dogâ€“assisted walking, and 3 hours of blindfolded walking sessions guided by six different guide dogs.

Based on these findings, we developed GuideNav, a vision-only, teach-and-repeat navigation system. Similar to how guide dogs are trained and assist their handlers, GuideNav autonomously repeats a path demonstrated by a sighted person using a robot. The system builds a topological representation of the taught route, integrates visual place recognition with temporal filtering, and employs a learned relative pose estimator to provide input to the controllerâ€”all without relying on heavy, power-hungry sensors such as LiDAR.

In field tests, GuideNav consistently achieved kilometer-scale route following across five outdoor environments, maintaining reliability despite noticeable appearance changes between teach and repeat runs. A user study with 3 guide dog handlers and one guide dog trainer confirmed the systemâ€™s feasibility, marking (to our knowledge) the first demonstration of a quadruped mobile system retrieving a path in a manner comparable to guide dogs.

Dataset Description

GuideData (Handlerâ€“Guide dog Interaction) dataset captures how blind and low-vision (BLV) individuals work with guide dogs in real-world contexts. It includes:

  • Semi-structured interviews with guide dog handlers and trainers.
  • Video observations of daily navigation and matching training sessions.
  • Blindfolded walking sessions with trained guide dogs, documenting both capabilities and limitations.
  • Supplementary data such as pulling-force measurements and handler/trainer feedback on robotic guide dog systems.

All recordings are anonymized, with participant identities protected and faces blurred. This is the first open dataset of its kind, providing rich insights into guide dogâ€“assisted navigation to inform the design of robotic guide dog systems and accessible navigation technologies.

Guide Dog Handler Interview

We also include semi-structured interview sessions with guide dog handlers to give insights into the human-centric development of future guide dog robots.
